Autonomous Rescue Operations Robot

Under Prof. Necmiye Ozay • Electrical & Computer Engineering (ECE) • University of Michigan • Jan – Apr 2024
Overview: An autonomous rescue robot for search and rescue scenarios. It uses ultrasonic ranging and a thermal sensor to navigate, avoid obstacles, detect heat sources (victims), and stop/alert with a buzzer, with closed-loop motor control for stable motion.

Role & Tools

  • Software/Firmware: C/C++, Arduino IDE, VS Code
  • Hardware: BeagleBone Blue, Arduino Nano, L298N motor driver, 74153 MUX, dual ultrasonic sensors, thermopile, buzzer
  • Test & Fabrication: Oscilloscope, Logic Analyzer, DMM, Bench Supply, 3D Printer, Soldering tools

Demo Video: Path-following and heat “victim” detection (the robot follows the course, finds the orange bottle (thermal target/person), and beeps on detection)

Results

~15 cmObstacle detection range (ultrasonic)
< 250 msHeat target detect-to-alert latency
Closed-loop Proportional control for stable turns
