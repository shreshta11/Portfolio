About Me!

Hi! I’m Shreshta, a junior in Electrical Engineering at the University of Michigan focused on power electronics, low-level circuit design, and energy-efficient computer architecture. I love building and exploring hardware - especially electronics for rockets and satellites, resilient systems for extreme environments like oceans and space, and embedded power systems that advance energy sustainability. I’m also excited about startups and about using space technologies and discoveries to improve life here on Earth!

Recently, I interned with the SEEDER Group at the National University of Singapore, where I explored hardware–software co-design for memory-centric accelerators. Before that, I worked on power systems at Quest Global.

On campus, you’ll find me working on rocket avionics or organizing outreach with MASA, performing Indian classical music with Michigan Sahana, jamming on piano and guitar, or playing basketball with friends.
